## Eligibility Criteria

Securing an [MBBS admission in Kazakhstan](/apply) requires meeting both the university’s internal standards and the foundational eligibility criteria mandated by the Indian medical authorities.

*   **Age Limit:** The student must have attained the age of 17 years on or before December 31st of the year of admission.
*   **Academic Performance:** A minimum aggregate of 50% marks in Physics, Chemistry, and Biology (PCB) in the 12th standard (10+2) board examinations for General category students. For SC/ST/OBC categories, the minimum is 40%.
*   **Mandatory Subjects:** English must be studied as a compulsory subject in the 10+2 curriculum.

## NEET Requirement

Is NEET compulsory for [MBBS in Kazakhstan for Indian students](/guides/mbbs-abroad-2026-guide)? **Yes, it is legally mandatory.**

As per the rules set by the National Medical Commission (NMC), any Indian student seeking to pursue medical education abroad and subsequently practice in India must clear the National Eligibility cum Entrance Test (NEET-UG).
*   **Qualifying Marks:** You only need to achieve the minimum qualifying percentile set by the NTA for your respective category. High scores are not required for abroad admissions.
*   **Validity:** The NEET scorecard is valid for a period of three years for international medical admissions.

## MBBS Fee Structure 2026

A major draw of Kazakhstan is financial predictability. Let's examine a realistic breakdown of [MBBS in Kazakhstan fees](/guides/mbbs-abroad-fees-comparison-2026).

### Tuition Fees
Depending on the university's prestige, annual tuition fees range from **$3,500 to $5,500** (Approx. ₹3 Lakhs to ₹4.6 Lakhs). Kazakh National Medical University and Al-Farabi are on the higher end, while SKMA and Semey offer more affordable brackets.

### Hostel Fees
Government university hostels are highly subsidized. They generally cost between **$600 to $800 per year** (Approx. ₹50,000 to ₹67,000). Hostels are secure, centrally heated, and generally accommodate 2 to 4 students per room.

### Mess Charges
Almost every major university hosts an Indian mess run by specialized contractors. These provide three nutritious Indian meals daily, costing around **$120 to $150 per month** (Approx. ₹10,000 to ₹12,500).

### Living Expenses
Almaty and Astana are modern cities, but daily living costs (transport, internet, laundry) are very manageable, averaging **$50 to $80 per month** (Approx. ₹4,000 to ₹6,500).

### Travel Costs
Direct flights operate between New Delhi and Almaty (e.g., Air Astana, IndiGo). A one-way ticket typically costs **₹15,000 to ₹20,000**, making travel highly convenient.

### Total Budget Breakdown
For an Indian student completing the full 5-6 year program, the total absolute budget—including tuition, hostel, Indian mess, medical insurance, visa extensions, and basic living expenses—generally falls between **₹22 Lakhs to ₹32 Lakhs**.

## Student Life

Student life in Kazakhstan is an enriching blend of intense medical studies and cultural exploration. Cities like Almaty are cosmopolitan, featuring modern malls, cafes, ski resorts, and beautiful parks. Universities frequently organize sports tournaments, cultural festivals, and international medical conferences.

### Indian Community
You will never feel alone. Indian students celebrate Diwali, Holi, and Independence Day with immense enthusiasm. The large Indian community provides a strong support system for newcomers, helping them adjust to the new environment rapidly.

### Safety
Kazakhstan is widely considered one of the safest countries in the CIS region for international students. University campuses are equipped with 24/7 CCTV surveillance, biometric access controls, and strict hostel curfews. The local population is progressive and highly welcoming to foreigners.

### Accommodation
University hostels are the primary choice for first-year students. They are heavily guarded and centrally heated. From the second or third year, many students opt to rent private apartments in groups, which cost approximately $150-$200 per student per month, offering more privacy and kitchen facilities.

### Weather
Kazakhstan has a continental climate. Summers are warm and pleasant (25°C to 30°C). Winters are long and cold. However, the infrastructure is built to handle it—every single indoor space, from your hostel room and university lecture halls to public buses and malls, is heavily centrally heated. You only face the cold when walking outside.

## Frequently Asked Questions (FAQs)

### Q1: Is MBBS in Kazakhstan valid in India?
Yes, absolutely. Degrees from NMC-approved medical universities in Kazakhstan are fully valid in India, provided you clear the NEET-UG before admission and the FMGE/NExT exam after graduation.

### Q2: Is Kazakhstan safe for Indian students?
Yes, Kazakhstan is considered one of the safest countries in Central Asia. University campuses have strict security protocols, and cities like Almaty and Astana are highly modernized and secure.

### Q3: How much does MBBS cost in Kazakhstan?
The total budget for the entire 5-6 year program, including tuition, hostel, and basic living expenses, generally ranges from ₹22 Lakhs to ₹32 Lakhs depending on the university.

### Q4: Can I practice in India after MBBS?
Yes. After graduating, you must clear the FMGE or NExT exam, complete a mandatory 1-year internship in India, and register with the State Medical Council to practice legally.

### Q5: Is NEET compulsory?
Yes. The National Medical Commission (NMC) mandates that all Indian students must qualify for the NEET-UG exam to study medicine abroad and practice in India.

### Q6: Which university is best?
The "best" university depends on your budget. Kazakh National Medical University (Almaty) is considered the most prestigious, while South Kazakhstan Medical Academy (Shymkent) is highly regarded for its affordability and FMGE results.

### Q7: Are the classes taught in Russian?

### Q8: Do I need to learn the local language?
While your studies are in English, learning basic Russian or Kazakh is necessary to interact with local patients during your hospital clinical rotations from the third year onwards.

### Q9: Is Indian food available?
Yes. All major medical universities have dedicated Indian messes serving vegetarian and non-vegetarian food. Indian groceries are also readily available in local markets.

### Q10: What is the exact duration of the course?
The course duration is 5 years of academic study followed by 1 year of mandatory clinical internship, fully complying with the latest NMC gazette guidelines.

### Q11: Do I need IELTS or TOEFL?
