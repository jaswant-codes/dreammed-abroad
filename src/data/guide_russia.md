## Eligibility Criteria

To secure [MBBS admission in Russia 2026](/guides/mbbs-abroad-2026-guide), Indian students must meet the following fundamental criteria:

*   **Age Limit:** The student must be at least 17 years old by December 31st of the admission year.
*   **Academic Score:** A minimum of 50% aggregate marks in Physics, Chemistry, and Biology (PCB) in the 12th standard board examinations for General category students. For SC/ST/OBC categories, the minimum requirement is 40%.
*   **Subject Requirement:** English must be a mandatory subject in the 12th standard.

## NEET Requirements

Is NEET compulsory? Yes. The National Medical Commission (NMC) mandates that any Indian student wishing to study medicine abroad and later practice in India must qualify the NEET-UG examination in the year of admission.

*   **Validity:** The NEET scorecard is valid for 3 years for students taking admission abroad.
*   **Cut-off:** You must score at or above the minimum qualifying percentile announced by the NTA for your respective category.

### Hostel Fees
Hostel fees in Russia are heavily subsidized, ranging from ₹20,000 to ₹40,000 per year. Rooms are centrally heated and usually shared by 2-3 students.

### Food Costs
Indian mess facilities are available at almost all top universities, costing around ₹10,000 to ₹12,000 per month. Alternatively, self-cooking can reduce food expenses to ₹7,000 per month.

### Living Expenses
Daily travel, internet, and personal expenses generally cost around ₹3,000 to ₹5,000 per month depending on the city.

### Travel Expenses
A one-way flight from Delhi to Moscow costs approximately ₹25,000 to ₹35,000.

### Total Budget Estimation
For a standard 6-year medical degree in Russia, an Indian student should budget an estimated **₹25 Lakhs to ₹35 Lakhs** covering tuition, hostel, and basic living expenses.

## Advantages of MBBS in Russia

*   **Infrastructure:** Massive hospitals, simulation centers, and massive libraries.

## Disadvantages of MBBS in Russia

*   **Climate:** The extreme cold during winters (often dropping below -15°C) requires adjustment.
*   **Language Barrier:** While classes are in English, communicating with local patients during clinical rotations requires learning basic Russian.
*   **Distance from Home:** 6 years is a long time, and flights can be expensive.

### Weather Conditions
Winters are severe, running from November to March. However, every indoor space—including hostels, classrooms, buses, and malls—is centrally heated. You will only experience the cold when walking outside.

### Safety for Indian Students
Is Russia safe for girls? Yes, absolutely. Russian cities are heavily policed, and university campuses have strict security, CCTV surveillance, and curfews. The local population is generally very respectful toward Indians.

### Indian Food Availability
You will never miss home food. Top universities have dedicated Indian mess contractors serving authentic North and South Indian meals. Furthermore, Indian groceries and spices are readily available in local supermarkets.

## Internship Opportunities

The 6th year of the MBBS program in Russia is heavily focused on clinical rotations. Students work in university-affiliated hospitals, assisting senior doctors in various departments like surgery, pediatrics, and gynecology. This hands-on experience is invaluable.

## FMGE/NExT Performance

Graduates from Russian universities have consistently performed well in the Foreign Medical Graduates Examination (FMGE). With the upcoming National Exit Test (NExT), Russian universities have already begun adapting their curriculum to ensure Indian students are well-prepared for the licensing exams.

## Career Opportunities After Graduation

A Russian MBBS degree opens doors globally.

### Can Students Practice in India?
Yes. After graduating, you must clear the FMGE (or NExT), complete a mandatory 1-year internship in India, and register with the State Medical Council.

### Can Students Practice in USA?
Yes. The degree is recognized by ECFMG. You can apply for the United States Medical Licensing Examination (USMLE) to pursue your residency in the USA.

### Can Students Practice in UK?
Yes. You are eligible to write the Professional and Linguistic Assessments Board (PLAB) exam to practice in the United Kingdom.
